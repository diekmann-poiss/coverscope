📚 Methodology

Overview

CoverScope measures living green vegetation coverage from a photo, entirely on device. Deterministic and reproducible - no internet or AI guessing required.

Core Method: Excess-Green Index

The primary detection uses the Excess-Green Index (ExG), a standard remote-sensing formula:

ExG = (2 × Green) − Red − Blue

This works because healthy green vegetation reflects strongly in green while absorbing red and blue light. High ExG = green plants; low/negative ExG = soil, walls, sky, non-vegetation.

Green-Dominance Check

In addition to ExG, we verify: Green > Red AND Green > Blue

This prevents false positives from bright red/blue objects that might have high ExG values.

Sensitivity

Aggressive (left): Lower threshold - catches faint green, more inclusive
Conservative (right): Higher threshold - only strong green, more exclusive

Scale Calibration

  1. Select two points of known real-world distance
  2. Enter the actual distance between them
  3. App calculates: metresPerPixel = realDistance / pixelDistance
  4. Plant area (m²) = plantPixels × (metresPerPixel)²

Health Index

Health = (Average Green Saturation + Average Green Value) / 2

Higher values = more vibrant, healthy vegetation. Best for comparing the same area over time.

Manual Correction

  • Add Mode: Paint missed plants
  • Erase Mode: Remove false positives
  • Corrections update calculations in real-time

Target Area

Focus analysis on a specific region (rooftop, garden bed, wall) rather than the entire image.

1m² Square

Drag the 1m² reference square anywhere on your calibrated image for instant coverage % of that exact patch.

Cubic Volume

  1. Place 1m² square on target area
  2. Enter plant heights at 4 corners + center
  3. Volume (m³) = Average Height × Coverage % × 1m²
